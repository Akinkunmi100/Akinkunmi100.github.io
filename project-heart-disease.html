Heart Disease Prediction

Built an ensemble machine learning system to predict heart disease risk using patient clinical data, achieving 80.9% accuracy through XGBoost, Random Forest, and stacking techniques.

DSN Bootcamp 2024 Project

Predictive Analytics for Early Heart Disease Detection

The Challenge

The challenge revolves around creating a sophisticated predictive model to determine the likelihood of an individual having heart disease. As one of the leading causes of global mortality, detecting heart disease in its early stages is pivotal for enhancing patient outcomes and halting its progression.

Conventional diagnostic methods often come with substantial costs and time requirements. Thus, there exists a pressing need for a cutting-edge predictive model that can evaluate the risk of heart disease utilizing easily accessible patient information.

Using data on 10,000 patient records, the goal was to design and build a predictive model capable of accurately determining the probability of an individual having heart disease.

Why It Matters

  • Early Intervention: Timely prediction aids in prevention and better outcomes
  • Cost Reduction: Reduces unnecessary diagnostic procedures
  • Resource Optimization: Targets healthcare resources effectively
  • Public Health Impact: Aggregated insights drive targeted health efforts

The Approach

I developed a comprehensive machine learning pipeline using Python, implementing multiple algorithms and ensemble techniques:

1. Data Preprocessing

Concatenated train/test data for unified processing, handled missing values, performed exploratory data analysis with histograms and correlation analysis.

2. Feature Engineering

Analyzed 14 clinical features including Age, Blood Pressure (trestbps), Cholesterol (chol), Max Heart Rate (thalach), and others. Applied StandardScaler for normalization.

3. Model Development

Implemented and tuned multiple algorithms:

  • Random Forest — Best performer with RandomizedSearchCV tuning
  • XGBoost — Gradient boosting for improved accuracy
  • LightGBM — Fast gradient boosting
  • AdaBoost — Adaptive boosting ensemble

4. Ensemble Stacking

Combined Random Forest, XGBoost, and AdaBoost using StackingClassifier with a Random Forest meta-model and 5-fold cross-validation for robust predictions.

Key Results

80.9% Mean Accuracy
10-Fold Cross Validation
10K Records Analyzed
4 ML Algorithms

Technical Highlights

  • Hyperparameter Tuning: Used RandomizedSearchCV with parameters for n_estimators, max_depth, min_samples_split, and min_samples_leaf
  • Cross-Validation: Implemented 10-fold K-Fold validation for robust performance estimation
  • Ensemble Learning: Used StackingClassifier combining RF, XGBoost, and AdaBoost with 5-fold CV blending
  • Comprehensive EDA: Univariate and bivariate analysis using seaborn and matplotlib visualizations

Tech Stack
